Non-Negotiable Productivity Rules
  • 1
    Phone goes in another room during study blocks.Not face-down. Not on silent. In another room. This single habit is worth 30% more retention.
  • 2
    Pomodoro 50/10 — not 25/5.You're doing IB + uni courses. 50 minutes deep work, 10 minutes completely off-screen.
  • 3
    Lights out by 11:30PM on school nights.Sleep deprivation tanks Physics performance more than anything else. Protect it like a class.
  • 4
    Wednesday is your flex day — decide every Sunday.Check your weekly review: behind on something → Option A (study). On track + coach meeting → Option B (college apps). Never waste it.
  • 5
    Start every session by writing one specific goal."Finish 2 SAT math sections" beats "study SAT." Specificity kills procrastination.
  • 6
    Physics and Literature get your sharpest hours.If tired → do Business or English. Never waste peak mental state on easy subjects.
  • 7
    Every chat with Claude = accountability check-in.Open every conversation with your study report before asking for help.
  • 8
    Review time ≥ practice time for SAT.Don't cram full tests constantly. Always review mistakes longer than you practiced.
  • 9
    Never two heavy cognitive days back-to-back.Rhythm: Deep → Medium → Deep → Light → Power → Reset. Friday is your light day. Protect it.
How to Check In With Claude

Every time you start a conversation, open with a quick update. This is the protocol:

"Yesterday I studied [SUBJECT] for [X] hours. I covered [TOPIC]. I did / did not complete my scheduled block. Today I need help with [X]."

Based on your check-in, Claude will flag if you're falling behind, adjust priorities, and help with whatever you need — content review, practice problems, essay feedback, SAT strategy, or college app prep.
